# Brand Injection Guide: Bachelorette Party on a Yacht in Miami

**Article file:** `components/blog/BacheloretteArticle.tsx`
**Translation namespace:** `bach.*`
**Target audience:** Maid of Honor planning a bachelorette, Bride-to-be researching, wedding party members
**Conversion goal:** Direct /book click or experience page click
**Funnel stage:** High-intent research → ready to book within days/weeks

---

## Brand Voice for Bachelorette Audience

**Core tone:** Fun, warm, slightly conspiratorial (the MOH is in on the plan), empowering, effortless luxury.

**What to lead with:** Not the yacht. The _feeling_. The yacht is the vehicle. The feeling is the bride having the best day of her life with her best friends, no logistics stress, no bad table at a restaurant, no cover charge line.

**Voice modifiers:**

- "We'll handle everything so you can focus on celebrating her."
- "Your rules, your music, your moment."
- "This is the bachelorette she's still talking about at the 10-year anniversary."
- Never use "charter" in the first 3 paragraphs — say "private yacht" or "the boat is yours."
- Lead with the experience, mention Emerald Eyes by name in paragraph 2+ after establishing the vision.
- Use "she" and "her" freely — this audience is planning _for_ the bride.

**What to avoid:**

- Corporate language ("we offer", "our services include")
- Overly nautical terminology in early sections (anchors, starboard, etc.)
- Pricing-first framing — earn the price reveal after building desire
- Passive voice on CTA copy

## Section-by-Section Injection Points

### Hero Section

**Translation keys:** `bach.heroTitle`, `bach.intro1`, `bach.intro2`, `bach.quick.*`

**Position:** Emerald Eyes is the _only_ answer when someone asks "where should we do the bachelorette in Miami?"

**Suggested `bach.heroTitle`:**

> "Bachelorette Party on a Yacht in Miami: The Complete Guide"

This is the SEO title — keep it factual. The brand magic goes in `bach.intro1`.

**Suggested `bach.intro1` (the hook — where EE gets established):**

> "Forget the nightclub. Forget the beach resort. Miami's best bachelorette parties happen on the water — just your crew, open skies, and no strangers allowed. With Emerald Eyes Miami, the entire yacht is yours from the moment you step aboard."

**Suggested `bach.intro2` (reinforce, add value signal):**

> "We've hosted hundreds of bachelorette parties on Biscayne Bay. This guide covers everything — from the perfect itinerary to what the bride-to-be should wear — so the MOH can plan the unforgettable day she deserves."

**Quick Facts card — make the value concrete:**

- `bach.quick.group` → "6 to 13 guests — the whole bridal party"
- `bach.quick.duration` → "4 to 8 hours — full day or golden hour cruise"
- `bach.quick.route` → "Haulover Sandbar, Star Island, Biscayne Bay, downtown skyline"
- `bach.quick.starting` → "Starting at $2,500 — splits to ~$250/person for 10 guests"

**Hero image:** `sax.jpg` is perfect — live saxophone is THE bachelorette signal image. Keep it.

**Author byline:** Sasha's byline ("By Sasha, Partner & Director of Operations") is already in the code. This is a high-trust signal for this audience — leave it prominent, it's the "insider who's done this 100 times" voice.

**Translation keys:** `bach.why.title`, `bach.why.p1–p3`, `bach.why.privacy.*`, `bach.why.views.*`, `bach.why.freedom.*`, `bach.why.tag1–3`

### FAQ Section

**Translation keys:** `bach.faq.title`, `bach.faq.q1–8`, `bach.faq.a1–8`

**Injection strategy:** Every answer is an opportunity to reinforce EE's "we handle everything" message. Don't be neutral — be confident and specific. These are the exact objections standing between a reader and a booking.

**Suggested FAQ copy:**

`bach.faq.q1` → "Can we bring our own drinks?"
`bach.faq.a1` → "Yes — Emerald Eyes charters are BYOB. We provide ice, coolers, and crystal glassware. Bring your favorite champagne, wine, seltzers, or non-alcoholic options. We'll keep everything cold."

`bach.faq.q2` → "How many guests can come?"
`bach.faq.a2` → "Our charters accommodate 6 to 13 guests. For groups larger than 13, contact us — we can sometimes arrange multiple vessels or discuss options for your group size."

`bach.faq.q3` → "What if someone gets seasick?"
`bach.faq.a3` → "Biscayne Bay is a protected bay with calm, flat water — nothing like open ocean conditions. Most guests have no issues at all. That said, anyone prone to motion sensitivity should take Dramamine before boarding and let us know in advance."

`bach.faq.q4` → "Can we play our own music?"
`bach.faq.a4` → "Absolutely. Every charter includes a premium Bluetooth sound system. Connect your playlist before you leave the dock. Your music, your volume — the captain isn't going to change the station."

`bach.faq.q5` → "Do we need to decorate the yacht ourselves?"
`bach.faq.a5` → "Not if you add our custom decoration package. Tell us the aesthetic — balloons, florals, banners, bride-to-be setup — and our team handles it before you arrive. You board a fully decorated yacht."

`bach.faq.q6` → "Is the live saxophone player available every weekend?"
`bach.faq.a6` → "Our saxophonist is available most weekends but books up fast for peak season (May–September). We strongly recommend adding this at the time of booking, not as a later add-on."

`bach.faq.q7` → "What happens if there's bad weather?"
`bach.faq.a7` → "Safety is always the priority. If conditions are unsafe for departure, we'll rebook you at no penalty. Miami weather is generally excellent, and our team monitors forecasts closely. We'll communicate with you in advance if anything looks concerning."

`bach.faq.q8` → "How do we book?"
`bach.faq.a8` → "Visit /book to check availability and reserve your date. You can also reach out directly — Sasha and the team respond quickly and can answer any questions before you commit."
