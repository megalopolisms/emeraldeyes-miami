## 5. Content Gap Analysis

The following are **high-value topics that no competitor covers well.** These are direct opportunities for Emerald Eyes to own featured snippets and PAA boxes.

### Gap 1: Tipping Etiquette (Specific Numbers)

- Most competitors say "tip the captain" without specifics
- **Opportunity:** State explicitly: 15–20% of charter fee; cash preferred; hand to captain who splits with crew; for a $3,000 charter, budget $450–$600
- Targets: "how much to tip on yacht charter miami," "yacht gratuity miami," "is tipping mandatory on a yacht"

### Gap 2: What Happens If Weather is Bad

- Very few articles explain what actually happens — most skip it entirely
- **Opportunity:** Explain the captain's authority, what "unsafe weather" means (winds >25 knots, lightning), that overcast/drizzle doesn't cancel, and Emerald Eyes' specific policy (reschedule at no cost)
- Targets: "miami yacht charter weather policy," "yacht cancelled due to weather miami," "what if it rains yacht rental miami"

### Gap 3: The 13-Person Coast Guard Rule Explained for Beginners

- Most articles mention it but don't explain _why_ or what to do for larger groups
- **Opportunity:** Clearly explain uninspected vs. inspected vessels; what "6-pack" rule means; how to handle groups of 14–25 (Coast Guard-inspected vessels or multi-yacht booking)
- Targets: "how many people can fit on a yacht miami," "yacht capacity miami limit" + internal link to Coast Guard article

### Gap 4: Bareboat vs. Crewed Charter — Beginner-Friendly Explanation

- Competitors either don't explain it or over-complicate it
- **Opportunity:** Simple 3-sentence explanation; strongly recommend crewed for all first-timers; explain what license is required for bareboat (Florida boating certification); gently steer toward Emerald Eyes' captained offering
- Targets: "bareboat vs crewed charter miami," "do you need a license to rent a yacht miami"

### Gap 5: Seasickness Tips

- Almost completely absent from Miami-specific competitor content
- **Opportunity:** Short section with practical tips (fresh air, horizon focus, Dramamine, eat before, no alcohol, catamarans rock less)
- Targets: "seasickness miami yacht charter," "what to do if seasick on yacht miami"

### Gap 6: Avoiding Scams / Choosing a Legitimate Company

- One competitor article exists but is thin
- **Opportunity:** Checklist of red flags (no USCG license, no contract, payment only in cash/Venmo, no physical address, prices that seem too low)
- Targets: "how to avoid yacht rental scams miami," "is [company] legitimate miami yacht"

### Gap 7: The First-Timer's Packing List (Specific to Miami)

- Competitors list items but no one has created a scannable, shareable checklist
- **Opportunity:** Formatted checklist (markdown-style in content, or a styled component) covering: essentials, clothing, sun protection, prohibited items
- Targets: "what to bring on a yacht charter miami checklist," "miami yacht rental packing list"

### Gap 8: The Best Time of Year + Booking Lead Time

- Exists in competitor content but scattered
- **Opportunity:** Consolidated section: peak (Nov–Apr), shoulder (May–June), off-peak (Jul–Oct), hurricane season awareness; specific booking lead times by season
- Targets: "best time to rent yacht miami," "how far ahead to book yacht miami"

### Gap 9: What "All-Inclusive" Actually Means in Miami

- Pricing transparency is a key anxiety point; the "all-inclusive vs plus expenses" distinction is genuinely confusing for beginners
- **Opportunity:** Clear paragraph explaining both structures with examples; list what Emerald Eyes' base price includes vs. optional add-ons
- Targets: "what is included in yacht charter miami," "all inclusive yacht charter miami"

### Gap 10: Music / Entertainment on Board

- Extremely common beginner question; essentially absent from competitor guides
- **Opportunity:** Short FAQ answer: yes, Bluetooth audio, can connect own playlist, can add DJ as upgrade
- Targets: "can you play music on a yacht rental miami," "yacht with DJ miami"
