## Content Gaps & Opportunities

These are topics competitors cover poorly or not at all — high opportunity for Emerald Eyes:

### 1. Transparent All-In Pricing (No Hidden Fees)

Most competitors show base rates but bury gratuity (15–20%, often $750–$1,500 on a $5,000 charter), fuel surcharges for routes outside Biscayne Bay, and platform/booking fees. **Opportunity:** Publish a fully transparent "what you actually pay" breakdown. This is a strong trust signal and differentiator.

### 2. Per-Person Cost Calculator Angle

Competitors never frame pricing as per-person. Splitting a $3,000 charter 10 ways = $300/person — cheaper than a nightclub table. **Opportunity:** A comparison chart (yacht charter vs nightclub bottle service vs hotel pool day club) showing value is highly shareable and not done by any competitor.

### 3. Seasonal Booking Calendar with Event Surcharges

Competitors mention "peak season" vaguely. None publish a concrete calendar showing: Art Basel (Dec) +20–30%, Ultra Music Festival (March) +15–25%, Formula 1 (May) +20–35%, Memorial Day/July 4th/New Year's Eve surge pricing. **Opportunity:** A month-by-month pricing calendar ranks for "miami yacht charter [month]" queries and earns backlinks.

### 4. Route-Specific Pricing Breakdown

Nobody breaks down what different routes cost in fuel surcharges: Biscayne Bay / Sandbar (included in base) vs. Haulover Inlet (minor surcharge) vs. Florida Keys (major surcharge) vs. Bimini, Bahamas (overnight/multi-day pricing). **Opportunity:** A "Where Can You Go" pricing page captures long-tail destination queries.

### 5. Captain-Only vs. Full-Crew Pricing

Most competitors don't explain the difference between a captain-only charter (you handle food, drinks, setup) vs. a full-crew charter (steward, bartender, deckhand included). Price difference is typically $300–$800 for crew. **Opportunity:** A dedicated "captain-only vs. full crew" section captures searchers researching crew options.

### 6. Yacht Type Comparison with Prices

Very few competitors compare motor yacht vs. catamaran vs. sailboat pricing side by side for Miami. **Opportunity:** A comparison table (vessel type → typical size → hourly rate → best for → what's included) is highly linkable and a featured snippet candidate.

### 7. "Deposit and Cancellation Policy" Content

Searchers frequently worry about losing deposits. Almost no competitor covers cancellation policy, deposit amounts (typically 25–50%), or refund conditions. **Opportunity:** Clear cancellation/deposit content reduces friction in the booking funnel.

### 8. First-Time Charter Guide with Pricing Context

New-to-boating customers need hand-holding: what to wear, what to bring, tipping etiquette, what questions to ask before booking. This content type earns trust, social shares, and "best yacht charter guide miami" rankings.

## Schema Markup Recommendations

### FAQPage Schema — Questions to Include:

1. **How much does a Miami yacht charter cost?**
   - Answer: Miami yacht charters typically range from $1,500 for a 4-hour private charter on a smaller vessel to $15,000 or more for a full day on a luxury yacht. The average 4-hour private charter costs around $2,200–$3,500.

2. **How much does a yacht charter in Miami cost per hour?**
   - Answer: Hourly rates in Miami range from $100–$300/hour for smaller boats, $300–$600/hour for mid-size yachts, and $600–$2,000+/hour for luxury and superyachts. Most charters require a minimum of 3–4 hours.

3. **Is a captain included in the Miami yacht charter price?**
   - Answer: Yes. All licensed charter yachts in Miami include a USCG-licensed captain as part of the base price. You do not need a boating license to charter a yacht in Miami.

4. **How much is gratuity on a Miami yacht charter?**
   - Answer: The standard tip for yacht crew in Miami is 15–20% of the charter price. On a $3,000 charter, budget an additional $450–$600 for gratuity. Gratuity is not included in the quoted price and is given directly to the crew.

5. **What is included in a Miami yacht charter price?**
   - Answer: Most Miami yacht charters include the vessel, a licensed captain, basic safety equipment, fuel for local Biscayne Bay cruising, ice, and bottled water. Gratuity, catering, alcohol, extended fuel for routes outside the bay, and water toys are typically extra.

6. **What is the cheapest yacht charter available in Miami?**
   - Answer: The most affordable private yacht charters in Miami start around $600–$800 for a 3-hour charter on a 30–40ft vessel. Budget boat rentals via platforms like GetMyBoat or Boatsetter can start lower, but private chartered yachts with a captain begin around $600–$1,000 for shorter durations.

7. **How much does a full-day yacht charter cost in Miami?**
   - Answer: A full-day (8-hour) yacht charter in Miami ranges from $2,500 for a smaller vessel to $12,000–$15,000 for a large luxury yacht. The sweet spot for most groups is a 50–65ft yacht, running $4,000–$7,000 for a full day.

8. **When is the cheapest time to charter a yacht in Miami?**
   - Answer: The most affordable time to charter a yacht in Miami is during the off-season (June–October) and on weekdays (Monday–Thursday). You can save 20–40% compared to peak season weekend rates. Avoid major event weekends like Art Basel, Ultra Music Festival, and Formula 1.

9. **How much does it cost per person to charter a yacht in Miami?**
   - Answer: Per-person costs depend on group size and charter price. For a $3,000 charter split among 10 people, the cost is $300/person. For a $5,000 charter among 15 guests, it's approximately $333/person — often comparable to or less than a nightclub table or hotel pool day.

10. **Can you charter a yacht from Miami to the Bahamas?**
    - Answer: Yes. Day trips to Bimini (the nearest Bahamian island, ~50 nautical miles from Miami) are possible with the right vessel and clearance. Multi-day Bahamas charters (Nassau, Exumas) typically start at $10,000–$25,000+ and require advance planning for customs and provisioning.
